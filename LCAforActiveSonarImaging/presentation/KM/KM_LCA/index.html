Delay-and-sum (DAS) beamformer (BF)

Beamforming:
The process of combining signals from different elements
$z[n] = \sum_{m=0}^{M-1} w_m^*[n] x_m[n$-$\Delta_m[m]] = \mathbf{w}^H[n] \mathbf{X}[n]$
  • The weights $\mathbf{w}$ are predefined and (usually) used for all pixels in the image

DAS uses predefined windows

Minimum variance (MV) beamforming

  • Adaptively choose weights with unit gain in direction of interest, that minimize the contribution from other directions:

$$\min_\mathbf{w[n]} E\{|z[n]|\} = \min_\textbf{w} E\{ | \mathbf{w}^H \mathbf{X} |^2 \} = \min_\textbf{w} \mathbf{w}^H \mathbf{R} \mathbf{w}$$ such that $\ \mathbf{w}^H \mathbf{a}(\mathbf{k}_0) = 1,$ where $\ \mathbf{a}(\mathbf{k}_0)\ $ is the steering vector.

MV response to point target

DAS vs MV BF:
The MV BF passes the signal of interest with unity gain while the interfering signal is suppressed.

MV response to cyst/shadow target
DAS vs MV BF:
The MV BF allows large sidelobes in regions with little energy.

Challenges when using the MV BF

  • Find a good estimate for $R$ (the covariance matrix)
  • Make the MV BF robust:
    • Make it work for coherent signals
    • Attain
      • Narrower peaks than DAS
      • Steeper and more defined edges than DAS
      • BUT: Speckle as DAS
  • Reduce the computational cost.
  • Use it correctly!

Known problems   Solutions
  • Signal Cancellation
    • Coherent signals
 $\Rightarrow$
  • Estimate $\mathbf{R}$ by spatial averaging
  • Increase no. of image lines
  • Robustness
    • W.r.t. phase aberrations and sensor imperfections
 $\Rightarrow$
  • Diagonal loading
    • Estimate $\mathbf{R}$ as $\mathbf{\hat{R}} = \mathbf{\tilde{R}} + \epsilon \mathbf{I}$
  • Speckle statistics
    • Wants speckle to look as with DAS BF
 $\Rightarrow$
  • Averaging in time
    • $\mathbf{R}$ is averaged over one "signallength" in time
  • Many param's to adjust
 $\Rightarrow$
  • Exp: "One size fits all"
